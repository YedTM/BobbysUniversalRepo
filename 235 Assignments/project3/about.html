Proposal/Documentation Page

'Til The Very Bookend

High Concept

Fight through enemies to reach your favorite book and try to make it out in one piece.

Genre

Adventure, Shooter

Platform

Desktop web

Story

You play as Laslow the Magus, who is a bit obsessed with collecting books and tomes. He hears of a super rare edition of his favorite novel, "Troglodyte's Guide to Book Collecting", and needs to get his hands on it immediately. The only issue, the book is guarded by a lot of sentinels and Laslow will have to fight or avoid them in order to get his desired book.

Aesthetics

The graphic style, for both visuals and sound, will most likely resemble 8-bit games (less detail, but can still tell what things are meant to be).

Gameplay

The gameplay will be moving around the play area space by space using WASD and using the C key to pick up items. WASD and Space will be used during combat to move and attack respectively. The player will be told these controls at the start screen. The first combat will be unavoidable, but will be easier than the others to ease the player in.

Documentation Section

Process

The process used during the creation of this game involved a lot of trial and error. I would implement a new function or idea, it would fail, and then I would go on and fix it or scrap it. I also divided my creation process into goals to make my efforts more efficient. My first goal was to finish up the world part of the game. I focused heavily on the collision and pickup systems to ensure that they worked properly. My next goal was to finish the battle section, which also involved collisions, but also needed to have a system for projectiles moving. The final goal was local storage and how it would connect the world and battle parts of the game. This mainly came down to preserving positions and allowing certain powers to be used.

Sources

All sprites and music were created by me. The sprites were made using Piskel and the music and sounds were made using Noteflight (sorry if the link doesn't work I could only get the myScores section of the website due to being signed in).
